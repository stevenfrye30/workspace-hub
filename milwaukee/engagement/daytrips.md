# Day trips within ~90 minutes

All drive times are approximate and assume no traffic. All pins also appear on the interactive map under the **Day trip** filter.

## North lakefront
- **Cedarburg** (25 min) — historic stone-mill village. Shops, covered bridge, Cedarburg Wine & Harvest Festival in fall.
- **Port Washington** (30 min) — harbor town. Lighthouse, Fish Day festival (July), quiet beach, fried perch.
- **Sheboygan** (60 min) — bratwurst capital. Lakefront, John Michael Kohler Arts Center; Kohler village just inland (Destination Kohler resort, American Club).

## West / inland
- **Holy Hill Basilica** (35 min NW) — Discalced Carmelite basilica perched on a hill. Climb the scenic tower. Fall color is the signature visit.
- **Oconomowoc** (35 min W) — Lac La Belle + Fowler Lake; historic downtown; weekend brunch spots.
- **West Bend** (45 min NW) — downtown riverwalk, Museum of Wisconsin Art (MOWA), Regal Ware country.
- **Kettle Moraine State Forest — Southern Unit** (45 min SW) — glacial kettles + moraines; Ice Age National Scenic Trail passes through. Scuppernong Trail is the easy-access gem.
- **Lake Geneva** (55 min SW) — resort town on Geneva Lake; the 21-mile Shore Path is the best free activity. Yerkes Observatory nearby. Crowds in summer.
- **Horicon Marsh** (60 min NW) — 32,000-acre freshwater cattail marsh. Spring + fall migrations bring tens of thousands of Canada geese. Visitor center on the north end.

## South
- **Racine** (30 min S) — **kringle** is the reason to go (O&H Danish Bakery). Also Wind Point Lighthouse (Great Lakes' tallest active), Frank Lloyd Wright's Wingspread, SC Johnson Administration Building tours.
- **Kenosha** (40 min S) — HarborPark, free electric streetcar loop, Dinosaur Discovery Museum, Civil War Museum, Jelly Belly factory tour nearby.

## Longer but routine
- **Madison** (80 min W) — state capitol, UW campus, Memorial Union terrace on Lake Mendota, Dane County Farmers' Market on the Capitol Square (Saturdays, April–Nov — largest producer-only market in the US).
- **Chicago** (90 min S by car; **90 min by Amtrak Hiawatha** from Intermodal Station, 7× daily, ~$25 one way). Train is the move — no parking nightmare, walk out into the Loop. Millennium Park, Art Institute, Lake Michigan, every food you can imagine.

## A note on Door County
Door County (Sister Bay, Ephraim, Fish Creek) is the archetypal Wisconsin getaway — but it's 3+ hours from MKE. It's a **weekend trip**, not a day trip.

## A note on Green Bay
Packers aside, Green Bay is 2 hours. Doable as a day trip for a game but tight.
