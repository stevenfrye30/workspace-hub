# Community & meetups

## How locals meet people (anecdotal)
- Leagues: kickball, softball, bowling, curling (yes, curling — Milwaukee Curling Club is the oldest US curling club still operating).
- Run clubs: Badger Track Club, Performance Running Outfitters group runs.
- Bike clubs: Wheel & Sprocket group rides, Milwaukee Bicycle Collective.
- Trivia nights (many bars; Wed/Thu common).
- Volunteer: Hunger Task Force, Riverkeeper, Urban Ecology Center.
- Church / faith communities — still very active in MKE, many of all denominations.

## Interest-specific
- **Tech / startups**: gener8tor accelerator events, 1 Milwaukee, Startup Milwaukee Week (fall).
- **Makers**: Milwaukee Makerspace (Bay View).
- **Coworking**: Ward4, Capitol Center, Hudson Business Lounge.
- **Artists**: Var Gallery, Sculpture Milwaukee walking series (summer).
- **Outdoors**: Urban Ecology Center (3 branches: Riverside Park, Menomonee Valley, Washington Park). Memberships cheap, great programming.
- **Readers**: Boswell Book Company events, Milwaukee Public Library branches.
- **Gamers / board games**: Board Game Barrister stores + events.
- **Music scenes**: local indie at Cactus Club (Bay View), punk/metal at X-Ray Arcade (Cudahy), jazz at Caroline's or The Jazz Estate (East Side).

## Finding my people — checklist
- [ ] Join one neighborhood Facebook / Discord group
- [ ] Sign up for one league / recurring activity within first 30 days
- [ ] Attend one community event per month minimum
- [ ] Find a third place (café, bar, library) I become a regular at
- [ ] Identify 3 potential friend-candidates from activities and actually invite one to coffee

## Dating / social apps
- Standard apps work; Milwaukee's pool is smaller than Chicago's — expect fewer but higher follow-through.
- Bumble BFF surprisingly active in MKE, reportedly.
