# Healthcare

## Major health systems in MKE
- **Froedtert & the Medical College of Wisconsin** — academic hospital (Wauwatosa), trauma center, many specialty clinics.
- **Advocate Aurora Health** — largest network in WI; many primary care clinics across the metro.
- **Ascension Wisconsin** (Columbia St. Mary's, St. Joseph, Elmbrook) — Catholic system.
- **Children's Wisconsin** — pediatric hospital in Wauwatosa.
- **Milwaukee VA Medical Center** — Clement J. Zablocki VAMC (veterans).

## Setting up
- [ ] Verify insurance is in-network with chosen health system (check before picking a PCP)
- [ ] Primary care (PCP): request new-patient appointment early — 2–6 week waits common
- [ ] Transfer existing Rx to a local pharmacy (Walgreens, CVS, Metro Market pharmacy, local independents)
- [ ] Dentist: separate from medical; cold-call a few for new-patient openings
- [ ] Eye care / optometrist
- [ ] Mental health / therapist: tough market; may need telehealth (Headway, Grow Therapy, Alma)

## Specialists worth knowing exist
- MCW oncology / cardiology / neurology — among the best regional options
- Froedtert Birth Center (if relevant)
- Children's Wisconsin for pediatrics

## Urgent care vs. ER
- Froedtert NOW and Aurora urgent care walk-ins cover most non-emergencies. ERs get expensive fast.
- Closest ER to your neighborhood is worth bookmarking before you need it.

## Insurance notes
- Marketplace (healthcare.gov) open enrollment runs Nov–Jan typically.
- BadgerCare Plus is WI's Medicaid; income-based eligibility.
- COBRA gives option to continue prior employer plan 18 months after move if between coverage.
