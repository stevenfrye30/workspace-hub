# Driver's license, ID, vehicle registration

Official source: **wisconsindmv.gov** (WI Dept. of Transportation / DMV).

## Residency rule
- You become a WI resident when you take up residence with intent to remain. You then have **60 days** to obtain a WI driver's license and **60 days** to title+register any vehicle.

## Driver's license (new WI resident with out-of-state license)
Bring:
- Current out-of-state license (will be surrendered)
- Proof of identity (birth certificate, passport, or REAL ID docs)
- Proof of SSN (SS card, W-2, pay stub with SSN)
- **Two proofs of WI residency** (lease, utility bill, mortgage, WI insurance card — must show name + address)
- Payment (~$34 for 8-year license as of recent schedule — verify current fee)

Process:
- Make appointment at a DMV service center (Mill Rd., Locust St., Southridge, etc.). Walk-ins work but waits are long.
- Eye exam on site.
- Road/written test **usually waived** if transferring a valid out-of-state license.
- Temp paper license issued same day; plastic mailed in 7–10 days.

## REAL ID
- Decide whether you want REAL ID (gold star, needed for domestic flights after enforcement dates). Requires slightly more documentation but no extra fee.

## Vehicle title + registration
Bring:
- Current out-of-state title (or lease paperwork if leased)
- Bill of sale / purchase details if recently acquired
- Odometer reading
- Proof of WI auto insurance (minimum WI liability limits required)
- Emissions test if living in an emissions county (Milwaukee County is one — but most vehicles are now exempt; check)
- Payment: title fee (~$164.50) + registration fee (varies by vehicle)

## Plates
- Standard plates issued at the DMV. Personalized and specialty (Packers, UW, etc.) available.
- Annual registration renewal by mail or online.

## Voter registration
- WI allows Election Day registration at the polls (bring proof of residence).
- Online / by mail through myvote.wi.gov.
- Photo ID required to vote.
