# Culture & local identity

## Vibe
- Midwestern friendliness + working-class roots + strong neighborhood identities.
- "Cream City" (from the cream-colored brick made here).
- Deeply German, Polish, Italian, Serbian, Mexican, Black heritages — still visible in churches, food, festivals.
- Self-deprecating, hockey/football/beer-adjacent humor; not flashy.

## Festivals (summer is nonstop)
- **Summerfest** — "world's largest music festival," late June / early July, 11 days, lakefront.
- **Ethnic festivals** on the Summerfest grounds all summer: Polish Fest, German Fest, Festa Italiana, Mexican Fiesta, Irish Fest, Indian Summer Fest, Arab Fest, Black Arts Fest MKE.
- **State Fair** (West Allis, early Aug) — cream puffs are the thing.
- **Bastille Days** (July, Cathedral Square).
- **Holiday Folk Fair International** (fall).

## Identity touchpoints
- Fish fry on Fridays (Catholic-rooted tradition; almost every bar has one).
- Supper clubs (old-school relish tray / brandy Old Fashioned / relish tray / prime rib format).
- Brandy Old Fashioned is the unofficial state cocktail — sweet, muddled, topped with soda (not whiskey, not bitter).
- Packers > Brewers > Bucks in the hierarchy for many locals, even though the Packers are in Green Bay.
- "Bubbler" = drinking fountain. "TYME machine" = ATM (older folks). "Stop and go lights" = traffic lights.
- Milwaukee accent: subtle nasal Upper Midwest; more pronounced further north.

## Arts & music
- Milwaukee Symphony Orchestra (Bradley Symphony Center).
- Milwaukee Repertory Theater.
- Pabst / Riverside / Turner Hall / The Rave — live music venues.
- First Stage (children's theater).
- Milwaukee Film Festival (fall).
