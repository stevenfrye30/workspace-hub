# Weather & climate

Humid continental. Lake Michigan moderates extremes somewhat but also produces lake-effect snow and the "cooler near the lake" summer effect.

## Yearly pattern
- **Winter (Dec–Mar)** — cold, snowy. Averages 20s°F highs, teens°F lows. 40–50" snow/year typical. Windchill real; lake-effect events.
- **Spring (Apr–May)** — slow warm-up, wet. Lake keeps early spring chilly near shore ("cooler near the lake").
- **Summer (Jun–Aug)** — warm to hot. 70s–80s°F, humid, occasional heat waves. Thunderstorms common.
- **Fall (Sep–Nov)** — often the nicest season. Crisp, long color.

## Weather-adjacent culture
- Polar Plunge (New Year's Day, Bradford Beach) — people jump in Lake Michigan in January.
- Lakefront is noticeably cooler than inland in summer. Check both forecasts if inland.
- Salt on roads is aggressive — undercarriage rusts quickly.

- [ ] Heavy insulated parka (0°F rated)
- [ ] Waterproof winter boots
- [ ] Thermal base layer
- [ ] Wool socks (multiple)
- [ ] Snow shovel (if not rental)
