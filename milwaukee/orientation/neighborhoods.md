# Neighborhoods

Milwaukee proper is ~96 sq mi, split by the Milwaukee, Menomonee, and Kinnickinnic rivers. Lake Michigan forms the east edge.

## Downtown core
- **Downtown / Westown / East Town** — business core; split east/west of the Milwaukee River. Deer District (Bucks, Fiserv Forum) anchors the west side.
- **Historic Third Ward** — former warehouse district, now arts + shopping + Public Market. Walkable, riverwalk-connected.
- **Walker's Point** — south of downtown, industrial-turned-trendy. Heavy on cocktail bars, breweries, restaurants.

## East Side / lakefront
- **Lower East Side / Brady Street** — Italian heritage corridor; nightlife + boutiques.
- **East Side (UWM area)** — around UW-Milwaukee; younger, student-dense, lakefront access.
- **Shorewood** — village north of the East Side. Lakefront, highly-rated schools, walkable downtown.
- **Whitefish Bay** — further north, similar vibe, more family.

## North / west
- **Riverwest** — east of the Milwaukee River from North Ave. Artsy, diverse, bohemian, co-op grocery.
- **Bronzeville** — historically Black cultural corridor (N. Dr. MLK Jr. Dr.), ongoing reinvestment.
- **Wauwatosa (Tosa)** — inner-ring suburb. "Tosa Village" is the walkable core; State St. retail strip; Mayfair Mall edge.
- **West Allis** — blue-collar suburb; Wisconsin State Fair grounds.

## South
- **Bay View** — south of Walker's Point; residential + strong bar/food scene. Very popular with younger movers.
- **Historic Mitchell Street / South Side** — Latino cultural heart; Basilica of St. Josaphat.
- **Cudahy / St. Francis / South Milwaukee** — lakefront suburbs south of MKE proper.
