# History

## Founding & growth
- Site was Menominee, Potawatomi, and Ho-Chunk territory. "Milwaukee" derives from an Algonquian word (commonly given as "gathering place by the water").
- Founded 1846 by merger of three rival settlements (Juneautown, Kilbourntown, Walker's Point) — reason the street grid doesn't align across the Milwaukee River.
- Massive German immigration mid-1800s — "Deutsch-Athen" (German Athens). Also Polish, Irish, Italian waves.
- Beer capital: Pabst, Schlitz, Blatz, Miller all founded here. Hence "Brew City."

## Industrial era
- Heavy manufacturing: machinery, engines (Briggs & Stratton), motorcycles (Harley-Davidson, 1903), mining equipment (Bucyrus, now Caterpillar).
- Allis-Chalmers (West Allis) — agricultural/industrial equipment giant.
- "Socialist Milwaukee" — elected three Socialist mayors (Seidel, Hoan, Zeidler) between 1910–1960. Known for clean government, professional public services ("sewer socialism").

## Civil rights & demographics
- Father James Groppi led 1967–68 open-housing marches across the 16th Street Viaduct.
- Significant Great Migration Black community on the north side (Bronzeville historically).
- Ongoing reputation as one of the most racially segregated US metros; active discussion in local policy/journalism.

## Modern
- Post-industrial decline late 20th c. → current diversification: healthcare (Froedtert, Advocate Aurora), finance (Northwestern Mutual, Baird), manufacturing (Rockwell Automation, Johnson Controls), tech growth.
- Harley-Davidson still HQ here.
- Hosted 2020 DNC (virtually, due to pandemic) and 2024 RNC.
- Bucks championship (2021) major civic moment, Deer District built around Fiserv Forum.
