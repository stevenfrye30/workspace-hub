# Language

A close look at how language is written, sounded, and shaped.

Language is everywhere and rarely looked at. This world is a slow study of its parts: the scripts it settles into, the sounds those scripts stand for, the symbols gathered around writing today, and specific languages that reward attention.

Each section is added as it is read or learned, at the pace of reading.

## What is here

Three current threads, each taken up slowly:

**IPA.** The International Phonetic Alphabet, laid out so a reader can see and hear what a sound is.

**Arabic.** A close reading of one language — its script, its sound, and the shape of its grammar.

**Digital Symbols.** Notes on the symbols that have gathered around writing in the last century, and the ones still without names.

Around these, a broader view: how languages are structured, how they have been described, and what makes one worth long attention.

## What is not here

This is not a linguistics textbook. It is not a course. It is not a survey of all the languages of the world.

It is a small, particular study — the languages and systems I am reading or trying to learn, arranged so a stranger can follow.
