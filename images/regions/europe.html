A thousand years of churches, altarpieces, and — eventually — secular portraits, landscapes, and the individual human strangeness at the turn of the 20th century.

Book of Kells
Insular Celtic · c. 800 CE · Ireland
A Latin gospel book from monks on Iona and Kells. The ornament is so intricate that Gerald of Wales thought it "the work of an angel, not of a man."
Chartres Cathedral
High Gothic · 1194–1220 · France
Rebuilt in 26 years after a fire. Its stained glass — especially the "Chartres blue" — has never been matched; the recipe was lost.
The Arnolfini Portrait
Early Netherlandish · Jan van Eyck · 1434
A merchant and his wife in a Bruges interior. The convex mirror on the back wall reflects two more figures — one of them possibly the painter.
The School of Athens
High Renaissance · Raphael · 1509–1511
Plato and Aristotle at the center, surrounded by the Greek philosophical tradition. Raphael slipped portraits of himself and Michelangelo into the crowd.
David
High Renaissance · Michelangelo · 1501–1504
Seventeen feet of marble carved from a block others had given up on. David faces Goliath before the stone flies — tense, not triumphant.
Sistine Chapel Ceiling
High Renaissance · Michelangelo · 1508–1512
Four years, mostly alone, on scaffolding. Michelangelo considered himself a sculptor, not a painter, and complained about the job the whole way through.
The Night Watch
Dutch Golden Age · Rembrandt · 1642
A militia portrait transformed into a theatrical scene — figures stepping forward, a drumbeat, a girl glowing in the crowd. Not actually set at night.
Las Meninas
Spanish Golden Age · Velázquez · 1656
The Infanta and her attendants — but who is the painting for? The king and queen appear only in the mirror. Foucault wrote an essay on that mirror.
Girl with a Pearl Earring
Dutch Golden Age · Vermeer · c. 1665
Not a portrait but a "tronie" — a study of an imagined figure. The pearl itself is two quick strokes of white paint. We don't know who she was.
The Starry Night
Post-Impressionism · Van Gogh · 1889
Painted from the window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy, from memory. The village below is imagined; the swirling sky he said he could actually see.
The Scream
Expressionism · Edvard Munch · 1893
Munch wrote in his diary: "I sensed an infinite scream passing through nature." The sky's red may be from the Krakatoa eruption ten years earlier.
