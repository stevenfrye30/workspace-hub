Europe

Post-Roman — illuminated manuscripts to post-impressionism.
← All regions

A thousand years of churches, altarpieces, and — eventually — secular portraits and landscapes. These six span from Irish monks around 800 CE to Van Gogh in 1889.

Book of Kells
Insular Celtic · c. 800 CE · Ireland
A Latin gospel book from monks on Iona and Kells. The ornament is so intricate that Gerald of Wales thought it "the work of an angel, not of a man."
Chartres Cathedral
High Gothic · 1194–1220 · France
Rebuilt in 26 years after a fire. Its stained glass — especially the "Chartres blue" — has never been matched; the recipe was lost.
Sistine Chapel Ceiling
High Renaissance · Michelangelo · 1508–1512
Four years, mostly alone, on scaffolding. Michelangelo considered himself a sculptor, not a painter, and complained about the job the whole way through.
The Night Watch
Dutch Golden Age · Rembrandt · 1642
A militia portrait transformed into a theatrical scene — figures stepping forward, a drumbeat, a girl glowing in the crowd. Not actually set at night.
Las Meninas
Spanish Golden Age · Velázquez · 1656
The Infanta and her attendants — but who is the painting for? The king and queen appear only in the mirror. Foucault wrote an essay on that mirror.
The Starry Night
Post-Impressionism · Van Gogh · 1889
Painted from the window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy, from memory. The village below is imagined; the swirling sky he said he could actually see.
