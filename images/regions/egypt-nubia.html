Egypt & Nubia

Three thousand years along the Nile.
← All regions

Pharaonic Egypt and its southern rival Kush left behind monuments and portraits of startling precision. The pyramids alone span a thousand years — and Nubia built more of them than Egypt did.

Great Pyramid of Giza
Old Kingdom · c. 2560 BCE
Tomb of Khufu. 2.3 million stone blocks, averaging 2.5 tons each. The tallest human-made structure on earth for 3,800 years.
Bust of Nefertiti
Amarna period · c. 1345 BCE
Painted limestone, found in the workshop of the sculptor Thutmose at Amarna. Still in Berlin; Egypt has asked for it back for a century.
Mask of Tutankhamun
New Kingdom · c. 1323 BCE
Eleven kilograms of solid gold, inlaid with lapis, carnelian, and obsidian. The funerary mask of a boy-king who died at nineteen.
Karnak Temple Complex
New Kingdom onward · 2000 BCE – 100 CE
A city of temples at Thebes, built and rebuilt by thirty dynasties. The hypostyle hall alone has 134 columns.
Abu Simbel
Reign of Ramesses II · c. 1264 BCE
Four colossal statues guard the temple front. In the 1960s the whole complex was cut apart and moved 200 feet up to escape Lake Nasser.
Pyramids of Meroë
Kingdom of Kush · 300 BCE – 300 CE · Sudan
Over 200 pyramids, steeper and smaller than Egypt's, built for Nubian royalty. Sudan has more pyramids than Egypt — about twice as many.
