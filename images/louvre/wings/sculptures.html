European sculpture from Romanesque cloisters to Romantic marble.

Psyche Revived by Cupid's Kiss

Antonio Canova
1787–1793 · Marble · Denon Wing

Cupid bends over Psyche, who has fallen into a death-like sleep. Her arms reach up to draw him in. The composition forms a perfect X — walk around it and the two bodies rotate through a dance.

The Dying Slave

Michelangelo Buonarroti
c. 1513–1515 · Marble · Denon Wing

Intended for the tomb of Pope Julius II, a project that consumed Michelangelo for forty years and was never finished as planned. The figure is not dying but — perhaps — falling asleep, or surrendering to ecstasy. Michelangelo left the back deliberately rough.

The Marly Horses

Guillaume Coustou
1739–1745 · Marble · Cour Marly

Commissioned for the royal estate of Marly, where Louis XV wanted rearing horses at the entrance to his watering pond. Each is over 3.5 meters tall. They stood outdoors for nearly two centuries before being brought inside in 1984.
