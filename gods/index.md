# Gods

Pantheons across cultures, set beside one another.

This world is a cross-cultural atlas of gods — who was worshipped where, when, and alongside whom. Dozens of pantheons are held in a single view, so their family trees, their adjacencies, and their echoes can be read together.

Entries are added slowly, checked against sources, and grouped by tradition.

## What is here

At the heart of this world is a single interactive view — [a family tree and temporal map](./map/) — holding the pantheons together. Each tradition is shown with its family tree, its place in time, and its points of contact with others.

The view grows as traditions are brought in; gods sit alongside the ones they inherit from, the ones they are said to resemble, and the ones they simply share a century with.

## What is not here

This is not a theology. It is not a comparative religion course. It is not an argument that the traditions are the same.

It is a careful arrangement — the gods of many traditions placed where a reader can see who sits next to whom.
